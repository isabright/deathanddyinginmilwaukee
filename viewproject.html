Journey

With a total of 79 recorded instances, the most common metaphor present in the collected obituaries was DEATH IS A JOURNEY. This metaphor conceptualizes death as movement from one place to another.  While some instances of this metaphor focus on movement towards some end location (usually the afterlife of the deceased's religion, such as in the phrase "went home to the Lord"), other instances instead focus on the movement away from the world of the living with no clear end location specified. An example of this type of metaphor is saying the deceased "departed this life". The most common phrase In this category is the phrase "passed away". 50 of the 79 instances of DEATH IS A JOURNEY include that phrase.



New Life

The next most common metaphor in the collected obituaries was DEATH IS NEW LIFE with 65 recorded instances. This metaphor conceptualized death not as an end to one life, but rather the beginning of a new life (as determined by the deceased's religious beliefs). This commonly shows up as saying someone was "born to eternal life" on the date of their death.



Peace

The third most common metaphor was DEATH IS PEACE. This occurred in 61 of the collected obituaries. This metaphor views death as a state of peaceful existence, free from the pain and struggle of life. It often co-occurs with other metaphors such as SLEEP/REST ("rest in peace") and JOURNEY ("passed away peacefully"). When found on its own, it is often in the form "found peace". It also often occurs when the obituary discusses an illness the deceased struggled with prior to death.



Death

While not common, some obituaries do not use metaphoric language to talk about a person's death. This occurred in 15 of the recorded instances.



Rest / Sleep

While common in epitaphs based on previous studies, the metaphor DEATH ISREST/SLEEP does not occur often in obituaries. We recorded a total of 10 instances, none of which constituted the main metaphor of an obituary. When this metaphor did show up, it was often at the end, wishing for the deceased to "rest in (God's) peace".



Reunion / Meeting

DEATH IS A REUNION/MEETING often shares themes with DEATH IS A NEW LIFE in that it envisions the deceased as rejoining loved ones who have died before them(often spouses) to live together again. This metaphor occurred in 10 of the recorded instances. In two of these instances, instead of meeting a previously deceased loved one, the deceased was actually said to be meeting "sister death". Both of these individuals were part of the Order of Friars Minor.



Call From God / Reward

The metaphors of DEATH IS A CALL FROM GOD and DEATH IS A REWARD are both strongly connected to religion and the idea of a paradisical afterlife the deceased receives after they die. It occurred in 8 of the recorded instances. These metaphors can appear in the phrases "X was called home to God" and "X received their eternal reward".



Final Moments / The End

The metaphors of DEATH IS A FINAL MOMENT and DEATH IS THE END deal with the temporal aspects of death. They talk about death as the end of a life or discuss the moments of one's life that occur just before they die instead of referring to the death directly. This occurred 8 times, such as in the statement that the deceased's life "came to a (peaceful) end". Interestingly, this metaphor does not contain the euphemistic or consolatory effects seen in other metaphors.



Separation

The metaphor DEATH IS SEPARATION focuses on how death separates the deceased from their living loved ones. It occurs in 5 of the recorded instances. In the collected obituaries, it has a strong connection to DEATH AS A JOURNEY, as all the instances of this metaphor look at how the deceased "left us" or is "leaving behind" people.



No Mention

In some obituaries, the death of the individual is not mentioned at all. The obituary instead focuses on the life of the deceased. This occurs 4 times. In a way, these instances are the most striking example of the death taboo. Death is so unmentionable that it does not appear, even metaphorically in the obituary.



Freedom

The metaphor DEATH IS FREEDOM is related to DEATH IS PEACE in that it discusses death as being free from the struggles of living. In the 2 instances it  occurs here, the obituaries read that one is freed from "their burdens" and the other is "free of any pain".



Loss

DEATH IS LOSS is the least common metaphor seen in this collection of obituaries. It occurs only 1 time in the phrase the deceased was "lost to us". Similar to DEATH IS SEPARATION, it does not offer a euphemistic or consolatory approach.
